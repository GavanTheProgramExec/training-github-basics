# Parents for Scouting, Inc. (PFSI) — Code of Conduct

## 1. Purpose

This repository is operated by **Parents for Scouting, Inc. (PFSI)** as a **training and learning environment**.  
Our goal is to provide a **safe, respectful, professional, and youth-appropriate** space for learning GitHub and collaboration skills.

All participants are expected to act in a manner consistent with:
- The values of Scouting
- PFSI policies
- Basic standards of professionalism and respect

---

## 2. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All participants in this repository (adults and youth)
- All interactions including:
  - Issues
  - Pull Requests
  - Comments
  - Commit messages
  - Documentation
  - Any other collaboration space in this repository

---

## 3. Core Principles

All participants must:

- Be respectful, professional, and Scout-like
- Be patient and supportive of learners
- Keep all interactions appropriate for youth
- Follow PFSI policies and instructions
- Assume all activity is logged and reviewable

Youth protection and safety **always come before convenience or speed**.

---

## 4. Expected Behavior

You are expected to:

- Use welcoming, inclusive, and professional language
- Be respectful of different skill levels and backgrounds
- Give constructive, helpful feedback
- Accept constructive feedback gracefully
- Focus discussions on the training material and tasks
- Follow the documented workflow and instructions

---

## 5. Unacceptable Behavior

The following are **not allowed** in this repository:

- Harassment, bullying, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory, demeaning, or hateful language or behavior
- Sexual, suggestive, or inappropriate content
- Political advocacy or campaigning
- Sharing or requesting personal data
- Sharing or requesting passwords, API keys, or secrets
- Private one-on-one messaging for PFSI-related work
- Attempts to bypass review, oversight, or safety processes
- Any behavior that violates PFSI or Scouting policies

---

## 6. Youth Safety Rules (Critical)

- This is a **youth-safe environment**
- No private one-on-one communication between an adult and a youth for PFSI work
- All collaboration must happen in **public, logged spaces** (Issues, PRs, comments)
- No discussion of:
  - Youth discipline
  - Youth incidents
  - Youth medical or personal matters
- No posting of any youth personal information

---

## 7. Content Rules

This is a **training sandbox**.

Do NOT post:
- Real names of youth or families
- Contact information
- Rosters, advancement, or medical information
- Screenshots containing real data
- Secrets of any kind (passwords, tokens, keys)

Only use:
- Fake/sample data
- Training-only content

---

## 8. Reporting Problems

If you see:

- Inappropriate behavior
- Policy violations
- Safety concerns
- Personal data or secrets posted

Do **not** engage with the content.  
Do **not** copy it.  

Report it immediately using:
- The repository issue template, or
- By contacting PFSI leadership or the CTO.

---

## 9. Enforcement

PFSI leadership and repository maintainers may:

- Edit or remove content
- Lock conversations
- Remove comments or posts
- Revoke access to the repository
- Take any other action necessary to protect youth, participants, or the organization

Serious violations may result in **immediate and permanent removal** of access.

---

## 10. Acknowledgement

By participating in this repository, you agree to:

- Follow this Code of Conduct
- Follow PFSI policies
- Act in a professional, respectful, and youth-safe manner

---

## 11. Summary

> This is a learning environment.  
> Be kind, be professional, be safe, and follow the process.

The goal is not just to learn GitHub — it is to learn how to work **responsibly, safely, and as a team**.
